What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Every day is different, and even if you've been programming every day for 10 years your bound to still be learning new things now. From what I understand, programming can focus on working in groups or squads so having values, empathy and self-awareness play key roles in how you interact with your squad members. Recognising your values gives you a great idea of what is important to you so you can clearly communicate them to your team if you are comfortable. Otherwise, it gives you a good idea if someone crosses those the line with those values and you will be more prepared to communicate with the specific team member about what happened and how to move forward from there. Same with empathy, if you end up crossing over someone else's values without realising, they can come to you with their concerns and it can be communicated from there so you don't cross those lines again, instead of being mad or upset that someone is mad or upset with you, it can turn into a learning experience and move forward positively. Self-awareness is key to keep good communication skills when it comes to learning and programming. Instead sitting stuck with a problem for too long and going insane, reaching out to another student/teacher/team member for help will save you a lot of time, and will make for a better outcome than staying stuck on a problem for hours.
