Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

One of the stores I worked at, we would also fulfill online orders. One woman I worked with just didn't see eye to eye from the get go however it became more apparent when we started to do these orders together. We would both make occassional faults, and she would never own up to the faults she was making and blame it on myself or another co-worker. So it was hard for the manager to pin-point what was going wrong and why, so there would never be any progress towards making sure the mistake wouldn't happen again. When these things were happening I would try to talk to her myself to see what was going on and why the mistakes had been made, however she would get defensive immediately and wouldn't have a clear answer as to what was happening. I became frustrated only because I was trying to help her in fixing those mistakes and move forward. At the time I would relay the information to the manager, she would call in the staff member, they would talk and it would be "resolved". I think looking back, instead of having us doing different orders it might've been better if we worked together on orders so we could both see where each other was at and keep an eye on how things were being handled. If there were issues that would still pop up it would be more effective to schedule 15mins in a day where myself, staff member and manager would meet so the three of us could talk together about solutions to the problems and hear each other out at face value.
